The Institution

to last centuries.

We are the institution where the world's royal courts place their creative heritage into permanent, wearable form — made entirely within our own walls.

The Manifesto

Heritage is a discipline,
not a decoration.

Article I

Heritage is a Discipline

We descend from the past; we do not borrow from it. Every code traces to a verifiable lineage. Pastiche is forbidden.

Article II

Seven Maisons, One Standard

Autonomy within the codes — palette, mark, quality, provenance, packaging. The Council enforces.

Article III

Craft Before Calendar

We release when craft permits. The Quality Bench holds the right of refusal.

Article IV

Provenance is Our Currency

Every hide, fibre, stone, and metal has a named origin.

Article V

The Wearer is the Heir

Owned, repaired, inherited — not consumed. Lifetime restoration, always.

The Codex

The alphabet in which the House speaks.

The golden section (φ, 1 : 1.618) governs every panel, body, last, and setting. The eight-pointed Royal Star — where Islamic geometry and European heraldry converge — is the universal mark. Twelve sovereign colours. Three typographic voices. Changed only by unanimous vote, never by season.

The Provenance Standard

No anonymous material enters the House.

Every object leaves with a bound dossier: named artisans and grades, material origins by registry lot, cultural-axis attribution, edition standing, certification, and the House seal in oxblood wax. Never a printed sheet; never digital-only.

Built to Last 200 Years

Permanence is engineered, not hoped for.

The Codex is held by a committee, not a person. The Archive sits in a charitable trust. The Academy grows its own artisans. Royal relationships are with houses, not individuals — they survive succession.
