Heritage

House of Saud

The House of Saud's first state was founded at Diriyah in 1727, when Muhammad bin Saud became Emir; the Diriyah Pact followed in 1744. Its seat was the mudbrick At-Turaif district, a UNESCO World Heritage Site inscribed in 2010. Abdulaziz Ibn Saud unified the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia in 1932. King Salman reigns today, with Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman as prime minister.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the maison.

Najdi court crafts are UNESCO-inscribed: the gold-embroidered bisht of Al-Ahsa, Al Sadu weaving, Al-Ardah — the Najdi sword dance with drumming and poetry — and Arabic coffee. The Al-Ahsa Oasis, home of the bisht tailoring, was inscribed as World Heritage in 2018. Mudbrick building defines At-Turaif itself.

The Maison

What Diriyah makes.

Diriyah makes ceremonial tailoring and objects rooted in Najd: gold-thread cloaks, woven cloth and carved-plaster motifs. The palette takes ochre from At-Turaif's mudbrick, sable from the bisht and Al Sadu wool, and gold from the zari embroidery.
