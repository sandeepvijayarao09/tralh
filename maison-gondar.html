Heritage

The Solomonic Dynasty of Ethiopia

The Solomonic dynasty ruled Ethiopia from 1270, its emperors claiming descent from Solomon and the Queen of Sheba. In 1636 Emperor Fasilides founded Gondar as the imperial capital and raised the Fasil Ghebbi enclosure — today a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The dynasty was deposed in 1974 with the overthrow of Haile Selassie, closing seven centuries of rule.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Court and church craft produced processional and hand crosses of latticed gold and silver, made by lost-wax casting and filigree — the twelfth-century Lalibela Cross among them; tilet hand-woven cotton (shemma, habesha kemis); Harari silver from the walled city of Harar; and Ge'ez manuscripts illuminated on goatskin vellum, the Garima Gospels the earliest surviving illustrated Gospels.

The House

What Gondar makes.

Cast and filigreed gold and silver crosswork, hand-woven tilet cotton and illuminated motifs drawn from the Ge'ez tradition. The palette runs imperial gold and rock-red against cross-white: the gold of regalia, the ochre of manuscripts, the white of spun cotton.
