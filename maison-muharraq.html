Heritage

House of Al Khalifa

The Al Khalifa took Bahrain in 1783 under Ahmed al-Fateh, \"the Conqueror\"; his line settled the pearling island of Muharraq, the house's namesake. The house reigns today as the royal family of Bahrain under King Hamad. Its ground is Dilmun-old: a harbour and trade island inhabited since roughly 2300 BC.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Bahrain's natural Gulf pearls are prized for lustre, reputedly aided by freshwater springs beneath the beds; cultured pearls are banned, and Danat certifies each. The Pearling Path of Muharraq — seventeen buildings, oyster beds and Qal'at Bu Mahir — is UNESCO-listed (2012); Qal'at al-Bahrain, capital of Dilmun, was inscribed in 2005.

The House

What Muharraq makes.

Muharraq makes natural-pearl jewellery and mother-of-pearl objects, certified and rare by design. The palette is pearl-white, oyster and pale gold — the round pearl, the shell and coral-stone, and the warm light of the old island wealth.
