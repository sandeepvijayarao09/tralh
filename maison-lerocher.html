Heritage

House of Grimaldi

The House of Grimaldi has held the Rock of Monaco since 8 January 1297, when François Grimaldi — \"Malizia\" — took the fortress disguised as a Franciscan friar, a ruse recorded on the arms by two sword-bearing monks under \"Deo Juvante\". One of Europe's oldest reigning houses, it endures today under Sovereign Prince Albert II, son of Rainier III and Grace Kelly.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Le Rocher draws on Monaco's Belle Époque court heritage: Charles Garnier's Opéra de Monte-Carlo (Salle Garnier, inaugurated 1879), whose red-and-gold auditorium of gilded stucco and frescoes was restored with over eighty thousand gold leaves; the Order of Saint-Charles, founded by Charles III in 1858 and still the principality's highest distinction; and the Prince's Palace State Apartments.

The House

What Le Rocher makes.

Le Rocher makes fine jewellery, ceremonial-inspired objets and evening accessories — pieces echoing the Grimaldi lozenge and gold-leaf opulence. The palette feels sovereign and warm: deep Grimaldi red, silvered white and gilded Belle Époque gold.
