Heritage

House of Al Thani

The Al Thani, a branch of the Maadhid claiming Bani Tamim descent, migrated through Al Zubarah before settling Doha. Al Zubarah was an eighteenth-century pearling and trading town, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site inscribed in 2013. The 1868 treaty recognised Al Thani leadership; Sheikh Jassim is held the founder of the state in 1878. Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani has been Emir since 2013.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

The coast's crafts are pearl diving and dhow building — living maritime heritage. Falconry is UNESCO-inscribed, as is Arabic coffee, and Al Sadu weaving, which Qatar joined in 2025. Pearling underpins the Al Zubarah World Heritage listing, and patronage now runs through the Museum of Islamic Art and Mathaf.

The House

What Zubarah makes.

Zubarah makes sovereign-toned pieces: pearl and mother-of-pearl work, fine textiles and leather goods. The palette is led by Qatar maroon, the national colour, softened by the luminance of the pearl and the sand of the pearling coast.
