Heritage

House of Bourbon

The House of Bourbon has reigned in Spain since 1700, when Philip V — grandson of Louis XIV — succeeded the last Habsburg king. The dynasty endures under Felipe VI, reigning since 2014. The house takes its name from the Royal Site of Aranjuez, the Bourbon spring residence on the Tagus, whose designed landscape was inscribed by UNESCO in 2001.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Built on real Spanish court crafts: tapestry from Madrid's Real Fábrica de Tapices, founded by royal decree in 1720 and still weaving in the Goya-cartoon tradition; Toledo damascene, the centuries-old inlay of gold and silver into blackened steel; the lace mantilla and peineta of court dress; and Madrid leatherwork in the Loewe lineage, granted a royal warrant in 1905.

The House

What Aranjuez makes.

Fine leather goods and accessories, hand-woven tapestry and figured textile, damascene-inlaid metal pieces and lace — worked in a restrained crimson, ivory and gold palette, ceremonial yet quiet.
