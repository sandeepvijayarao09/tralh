Heritage

The House of Romanov

The House of Romanov ruled Russia from 1613 to 1917, reaching its imperial height across the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries from seats including Peterhof and the Winter Palace in St Petersburg. The dynasty ended in the 1917 Revolution; Nicholas II abdicated and the court commissions ceased — among them the House of Fabergé, whose work halted with the fall.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the maison.

Fabergé, supplier to the court from 1884, worked guilloché enamel over engine-turned gold and made the Imperial Easter Eggs — about fifty, between 1885 and 1916. The Peterhof and Ekaterinburg lapidaries perfected the malachite \"Russian mosaic\"; the Imperial Porcelain Factory, founded 1744, served the court; ceremonial dress used gold-thread embroidery and sable.

The Maison

What Peterhof makes.

Enamelled gold objects, malachite work and fine porcelain in the imperial idiom, with gold-thread and sable detailing. The palette sets banded malachite green against imperial gold and palace white: the green stone, the gilded ground and the snow-white court.
