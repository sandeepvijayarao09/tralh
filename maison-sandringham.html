Heritage

House of Windsor

The House of Windsor was founded on 17 July 1917, when George V renamed the royal house from Saxe-Coburg and Gotha to Windsor during the First World War. The maison takes its name from Sandringham House in Norfolk — the estate held in the monarch's personal ownership since 1862, not by the Crown. The dynasty reigns today under King Charles III.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the maison.

Court crafts run to real houses: Ede & Ravenscroft, royal-warrant robemakers since 1689; the Royal School of Needlework, coronation goldwork to the present; Savile Row bespoke, protected by the Savile Row Bespoke Association; Balmoral-sett tartan woven by Lochcarron of Scotland; and royal-warrant leather by Launer London.

The Maison

What Sandringham makes.

Sandringham makes ceremonial-grade tailoring and bespoke suiting, fine British leather goods, and gold-accented accessories — estate green and oxblood worn every day, gold reserved for finish and occasion. Restraint first, ornament earned.
