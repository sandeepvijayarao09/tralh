Heritage

The House of Habsburg

The House of Habsburg rose with the election of Rudolf I as King of the Romans in 1273 and held the imperial dignity for centuries from its Vienna seats, Schönbrunn and the Hofburg. The monarchy is historical: Emperor Karl I withdrew in November 1918, Austria became a republic, and the Habsburg Law of 1919 dethroned and exiled the dynasty. It does not reign today.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Vienna porcelain, founded 1718 by Du Paquier — Europe's second after Meissen — revived as Augarten in 1923. J. & L. Lobmeyr crystal, court purveyor from 1823 and pioneer of the electric chandelier. Or nué gold-thread embroidery of the Order of the Golden Fleece, a chivalric order founded in 1430, its fifteenth-century paraments held in the Vienna Imperial Treasury.

The House

What Schönbrunn makes.

Schönbrunn makes hand-finished porcelain and tableware, cut crystal, gold-thread embroidery and ceremonial objets — court pieces carried in ochre-yellow, imperial black and burnished gold, restrained, sovereign and unmistakably Viennese.
