Heritage

The Asaf Jahi Nizams of Hyderabad

The Asaf Jahi dynasty, the Nizams of Hyderabad, was founded by Asaf Jah I in 1724 as Mughal power waned in the Deccan. It ruled for two hundred and twenty-four years from the seats of Chowmahalla and Falaknuma palaces. After the seventh Nizam declined to accede at independence, Operation Polo annexed Hyderabad State into India in September 1948, closing the dynasty's reign.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Golconda was the world's foremost diamond source, yielding the Type IIa Koh-i-Noor and Hope. The court sustained Bidriware, silver inlaid into blackened alloy and GI-registered in 2006; Himroo and mashru silk-cotton brocade; karchob zardozi metallic-thread embroidery, often pearled; and nearby Pochampally ikat, India's first handloom GI, 2004.

What Golconda makes.
