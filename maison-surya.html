Heritage

The House of Mewar

The House of Mewar, the Sisodia Rajputs of Udaipur, is among the world's longest-serving dynasties, traced to Guhila ancestry and to Bappa Rawal around 734 CE, and famed through Maharana Pratap. It claims solar (Suryavanshi) descent — Surya, the sun. Its seat is the City Palace, Udaipur, begun in 1559. India abolished princely titles in 1971; the house endures as titular custodians, with succession currently disputed.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the maison.

Mewar's signature is its school of miniature painting — Krishna-lila, Ragamala and court scenes in mineral pigment lifted with gold, the largest collection held in the City Palace Museum. Allied court crafts are meenakari, the fusing of coloured enamels onto gold and silver, and kundan jewellery, gemstones set in refined pure gold over enamelled reverses.

The Maison

What Surya makes.

Surya makes miniature-inspired works, kundan-and-meenakari jewellery and enamelled gold objects. The palette sets festival marigold and the solar emblem against limewashed ivory and the lake-blue of Pichola — radiant, courtly and luminous.
