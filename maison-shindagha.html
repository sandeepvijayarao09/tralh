Heritage

House of Al Maktoum

The Al Maktoum descend from the Al Bu Falasah section of the Bani Yas. In 1833 some eight hundred of them, led by Maktoum bin Butti, settled Dubai and founded the line. Their seat lay at Al Shindagha on Dubai Creek, where Sheikh Saeed Al Maktoum House still stands beneath its wind towers. Sheikh Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum rules Dubai today.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Dubai's coastal crafts run from pearl diving and dhow building to the barjeel wind tower of Al Shindagha and Al Fahidi. Falconry is UNESCO-inscribed, as is Arabic coffee; the saluki hound is a heritage hunting companion. Pearling underpins the heritage that built the creek.

The House

What Shindagha makes.

Shindagha works in maritime materials: mother-of-pearl inlay, fine textiles and leather in creek and sea tones. The palette pairs the blue of the Gulf and Creek, the sand at its edge, and the shifting iridescence of mother-of-pearl.
