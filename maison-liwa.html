Heritage

House of Al Nahyan

The Al Nahyan rule Abu Dhabi as a branch of the Bani Yas, whose ancestral homeland is the Liwa Oasis on the edge of the Rub' al Khali. In 1761 a party from Liwa tracked a gazelle to a freshwater spring on Abu Dhabi island; settlement followed, and the seat moved there in 1793. The family holds the UAE presidency today under Sheikh Mohamed bin Zayed Al Nahyan.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the maison.

The court's living crafts are UNESCO-inscribed: Al Sadu Bedouin weaving, falconry, the gold-thread bisht cloak, and Arabic coffee, a symbol of generosity. The knowledge and practice of the date palm is also inscribed. Each is loom, hand and hospitality work carried from the oasis.

The Maison

What Liwa makes.

Liwa makes restrained pieces in desert tones: woven textiles, gold-thread tailoring and leather goods. The palette draws sand from the dunes, sable from Al Sadu warps and the falcon, and gold from the zari thread of the bisht.
