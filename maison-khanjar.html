Heritage

House of Al Said

The Al Said dynasty was founded in 1744 by Ahmad bin Said, who expelled Persian forces and unified Oman, beginning one of Arabia's oldest surviving ruling houses. It seats in Muscat and once reached Zanzibar. The house reigns today under Sultan Haitham bin Tariq, its emblem still bearing the curved dagger that names the house.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

The Omani khanjar — a curved silver dagger of belt, hilt, blade and scabbard — was inscribed by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2022, alongside the silversmithing of its engraved scabbards. Dhofar frankincense, traded for centuries, gives the UNESCO Land of Frankincense. The wrapped massar is woven in fine wool and silk; its blue Al Saidiya cloth is the royal family's.

The House

What Khanjar makes.

Khanjar makes silver objects, leather goods and frankincense-led fragrance — pieces carrying engraved silverwork and the dagger silhouette. The palette runs silver, oud-brown and royal red: cool metal, warm resin, sovereign colour.
