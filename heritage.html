Heritage as Structure

The world's living courts,
held as one.

Each royal house is a permanent pillar of the House — a living court whose craft lineage we gather, codify, and renew, never pastiche. Not under one roof, but each in its own name.

The Royal Houses

A grammar for every court.

The House bears no founding year of its own — it inherits the dates of the courts it unites, each a living lineage centuries old.

The Archive

A cultural institution, held in trust.

Every object, every material lot, every artisan credential is recorded permanently. The Archive is held by a charitable trust — it cannot be liquidated in any corporate event. The heritage outlives the House's every season.

Royal Patronage

Royal houses are stakeholders,
not sponsors.

Each allied royal house holds an annual right to nominate creators from its own tradition. Their year of work is attributed under that house's name and archived permanently — a living instrument of heritage, not a borrowed past.
