Heritage as Structure

Nine royal traditions,
held as one.

The cultural axes are not a theme; they are the permanent pillars of the House. Each is a verifiable craft lineage, gathered, codified, and renewed — never pastiched.

The Nine Cultural Axes

A grammar for every court.

Each axis carries its own silhouette, surface, and palette extension — expressed within the single House codex.

South Asia

Indian Royal

Mughal, Rajput, Maratha and South Indian courts — jadau, zardozi, katan silk.

Gulf & Levant

Arab Royal

Khaleeji, Alaouite and Levantine tradition — arabesque geometry, oud and amber, the bisht.

Europe

British Royal

House of Windsor, Highland and Tudor archive — tartan codes, Savile Row construction.

Europe

French Royal

The Bourbon and Versailles court — architectural proportion, toile, gilded surface.

Europe

Italian Royal

Savoy, Medici, Venetian republic — silk velvet, pietra dura, mosaic surface.

Europe

Spanish Royal

Bourbon-Spain and the Moorish palace — brocade, Toledo metalwork, escutcheon heraldry.

East Asia

Japanese Imperial

Chrysanthemum codes, Edo craft — yūzen, maki-e, shibori, restraint.

Southeast Asia

Thai & Siamese

The Chakri dynasty — gold thread, royal silk, temple geometry.

Africa

African Royal

Ashanti, Zulu and Swahili coast courts — kente, beadwork systems, bronze casting.

The Archive

A cultural institution, held in trust.

Every object, every material lot, every artisan credential is recorded permanently. The Archive is held by a charitable trust — it cannot be liquidated in any corporate event. The heritage outlives the House's every season.

Royal Patronage

Royal houses are stakeholders,
not sponsors.

Each allied royal house holds an annual right to nominate creators from its own tradition. Their year of work is attributed under that house's name and archived permanently — a living instrument of heritage, not a borrowed past.
