Heritage

The Chakri Dynasty

The Chakri dynasty has reigned over Thailand since 1782, when Rama I founded Bangkok and the Rattanakosin kingdom; ten kings have followed, the present being Vajiralongkorn (Rama X). Its sacred seat is the Grand Palace and Wat Phra Kaew, home of the Emerald Buddha. The royal Garuda, the man-eagle of Vishnu, was adopted as the national emblem by Rama VI in 1911.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Court crafts were revived through Queen Sirikit's SUPPORT Foundation: hand-woven royal Thai silk; benjarong, five-colour overglaze-enamelled porcelain gilded in the Lai Nam Thong manner, once exclusive to the court; nielloware (kruang thom) of black alloy inlaid in silver, revived from Nakhon Si Thammarat; and gold-and-silverware with damascene.

The House

What Garuda makes.

Garuda makes gilded porcelain, royal silk and niello-and-gold objects in the court idiom. The palette draws emerald from the Emerald Buddha, gold from Lai Nam Thong benjarong, and Thai royal blue — sacred, gilded and regal.
