Heritage

The Asante Court

The Asante kingdom of the Oyoko clan was formed in 1701, when Osei Tutu I and Okomfo Anokye united the Asante states and made Kumasi their centre. Sika means gold in Akan. The Asantehene, Otumfuo Osei Tutu II since 1999, reigns as a traditional monarch within the Republic of Ghana from Manhyia Palace. The Golden Stool, Sika Dwa Kofi, is said to hold the soul of the nation.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the maison.

The court's crafts are kente, the brilliant narrow-strip cloth woven at Bonwire and inscribed by UNESCO as Intangible Cultural Heritage in 2024, each pattern encoding a proverb; adinkra, symbolic calabash-stamp printing; and Asante goldsmithing and lost-wax brass casting, including the figural gold weights (abrammuo) that once measured gold dust.

The Maison

What Sika makes.

Sika makes goldwork, woven kente and adinkra-marked cloth and cast-metal objects. The palette crowns Asante gold with the saturated yellows, greens, reds and blues of kente and the black of ceremonial adinkra — proverb, gold and gravitas.
