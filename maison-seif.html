Heritage

House of Al Sabah

The Al Sabah have led Kuwait since about 1752, when the Banu Utub appointed Sabah I as ruler. Seated in the old town on Kuwait Bay, the house still reigns, its Emir drawn from the family. Its world is the meeting of desert and sea — Bedouin loom and pearling dhow — that shaped Kuwait before oil.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Al Sadu, the warp-faced wool weaving of Bedouin women, was inscribed by UNESCO in 2020; its geometric motifs record migration and identity, safeguarded since 1980 at Kuwait's Sadu House. The diwaniya, the men's gathering for counsel, was inscribed in 2025. Natural Gulf pearling and traditional dhow carpentry survive as living maritime craft.

The House

What Seif makes.

Seif makes woven textiles, pearl jewellery and leather pieces drawn from loom and sea. The palette is pearl, indigo and gold — the lustre of a Gulf pearl, the indigo of Sadu wool, the gold of the majlis.
