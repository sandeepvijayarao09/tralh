Heritage

Alaouite Dynasty

A sharifian house claiming descent from the Prophet, the Alaouites rose when Mawlay al-Sharif was declared sultan of the Tafilalt oasis in 1631; Mawlay al-Rashid then reunified Morocco, taking Fez in 1666. The dynasty reigns today under King Mohammed VI, who holds the title Commander of the Faithful. Tafilalt names the cradle, not the living surname.

Savoir-Faire

The craft beneath the house.

Zellij — hand-cut glazed tiles set into geometric stars — has adorned Fez architecture since around the fourteenth century. The Moroccan caftan, in brocade and silk with handmade braids, was inscribed by UNESCO in December 2025. Saffron-dyed babouche and natural-dyed leather come from Fez's Chouara tannery, within the UNESCO-listed Medina of Fez and its 859 CE university.

The House

What Tafilalt makes.

Tafilalt makes leather goods, caftan-inspired dress and tilework-patterned objects. The palette is zellij blue, saffron and gold — the cobalt of Fez tiles, the yellow of babouche leather, and the brocade gold of a sharifian court.
